About GeneSys
An experimental tool exploring the intersection of ancient Hebrew letter systems,
mathematical permutation, and molecular biology.

What is this?

GeneSys places the 27 Hebrew letters (22 standard + 5 final forms) onto a 3×3×3 Rubik's cube, sorted by topology: corners, edges, faces, and center. Through a configurable pipeline, you feed in sacred Hebrew text, apply classical ciphers and cube rotations, and translate the output into amino acid sequences — the building blocks of proteins.

The Pipeline

Source → choose Hebrew text (the Mezuzah's 713 letters, Genesis 1:1, or your own).
Transform → chain classical Hebrew ciphers (Atbash, Albam, Avgad, Milui, and more) and cube rotations driven by mathematical constants (π, e, φ, Fibonacci) or gematria values.
Mapping → convert the permuted letters to amino acids and analyze the resulting sequence for protein-like properties: molecular weight, charge, hydrophobicity, and more.

✡ 7 Hebrew Ciphers Atbash, Albam, Avgad, Achbi, Ayak Bachar, Milui — classical transforms from Jewish tradition
🧊 Permutation Algebra Every cube rotation is a permutation in S₂₇. Sequences compose instantly via matrix multiplication
🧬 Amino Acid Mapping 22 Hebrew letters map to 20 amino acids + stop codon. Customizable mapping with drag-and-swap
📊 Sequence Analysis Composition, molecular weight, charge, GRAVY score, hydrophobicity profile, FASTA export

The Question

Is there a meaningful mapping between Hebrew text and biological structures when mediated by systematic permutation? GeneSys doesn't claim answers — it provides the tools to explore. Share your discoveries via recipe URLs and see what emerges when ancient language meets permutation algebra.

Getting Started

Click ⚗ Pipeline below to open the transformation pipeline. Choose a source text, add cipher and rotation steps with the + button, then hit ▶ Run to see the cube animate through the entire sequence. The output panel shows the resulting Hebrew text and its amino acid translation with protein analysis.
